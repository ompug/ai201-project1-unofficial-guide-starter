Title: Student Notes - Choosing the Project 1 Domain
Source: Local file description: peer planning note based on the AI201 Project 1 assignment handout and grading rubric
Date: 2026-06-10
Category: planning
Rating: 5

Students who score well usually choose a domain narrow enough that five test questions have specific answers. A broad topic such as "campus life" becomes hard to evaluate because almost any answer sounds plausible. A better domain is a focused unofficial guide, such as housing lease warnings, dining hall rush-hour patterns, or how to complete CodePath AI201 Project 1.

For this project, the domain is an unofficial student survival guide for CodePath AI201 Project 1. The knowledge is valuable because the assignment instructions and rubric are long, and students can miss small scoring details such as sample chunks, out-of-scope refusal, and production embedding tradeoffs. It is hard to find through one official channel because the requirements are spread across milestones, hints, and grading categories.

The system should answer questions about the exact artifacts students need to produce: planning.md, README, source documents, chunking, retrieval tests, grounded generation, evaluation, and optional stretch features. It should not answer general CodePath administrative questions unless those details appear in the documents.
