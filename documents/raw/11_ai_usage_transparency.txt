The AI Usage section should describe at least two specific instances of AI tool use. Each instance should say what the student directed the tool to do, what input was provided, what output was produced, and what the student reviewed, revised, or overrode.

Good transparency sounds like collaboration rather than passive acceptance. For example, a student can say they gave Codex the chunking strategy and asked for an implementation, then changed the generated function to keep paragraph boundaries because the first version split sentences awkwardly.

Another strong example is using AI to draft evaluation commands, then manually checking whether each expected answer was supported by the retrieved chunks. The student remains responsible for verifying the output and documenting failures honestly.
