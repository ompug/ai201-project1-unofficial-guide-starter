Title: Must-Watch Anime Notes - Neon Genesis Evangelion for Psychological Mecha
Source: BFI feature on Neon Genesis Evangelion https://www.bfi.org.uk/features/neon-genesis-evangelion ; Netflix title page https://www.netflix.com/title/81033445
Date: 2026-06-10
Category: series
Rating: 5

Neon Genesis Evangelion is a must-watch because it is one of the clearest examples of a genre show turning inward. On the surface it is about teenage pilots fighting monstrous Angels in giant biomechanical units. Underneath, it is about isolation, depression, family pressure, fear of intimacy, and whether people can understand each other.

BFI treats Evangelion as a cult landmark worth explaining to new viewers, and Netflix's page gives the basic setup of Shinji joining NERV after the Second Impact. The series is essential partly because later anime, games, fashion, and internet discussion constantly reference it.

Recommendation note: use Evangelion for viewers asking for influential anime, psychological anime, mecha with symbolism, or shows that invite analysis. Warn that it is emotionally intense and that the ending is divisive, especially for viewers who want clean plot closure.
